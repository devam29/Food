Our Story

How It All Began

It started with a simple dinner. Five friends from different corners of the world — Nepal, India, Greece, and Albania — gathered around a kitchen table, each bringing a dish from home. What was meant to be a casual evening turned into something magical.

The aromas blended — cumin from India, timur pepper from Nepal, oregano from Greece, and mint from Albania. Stories were shared, techniques were exchanged, and a tradition was born.

Flavors United is our way of preserving these moments — not just the recipes, but the laughter, the stories, and the love that goes into every dish we make together. This website is our shared cookbook, our family album, and our love letter to the cuisines that shaped us.

Meet the Chefs

The Flavors United Five

Each of us brings a unique culinary heritage, a personal story, and an unshakeable love for feeding friends.

🇳🇵

Bikalpa Gautam

The Himalayan Soul — Nepal

Growing up in Nepal, Bikalpa learned to cook from watching his family gather around the kitchen every evening. For him, Dal Bhat isn't just a meal — it's home. His momos are legendary in the friend circle, and he's the reason everyone now knows what timur pepper is.

Bikalpa believes that food should be shared generously and made with love. His recipes carry the warmth of the Himalayas and the hospitality that Nepal is famous for.

Signature Dishes: Momo, Dal Bhat, Sel Roti, Chatamari, Thukpa, Choyla

🇮🇳

Dev Shree Saini

The Spice Virtuoso — India

Dev grew up surrounded by the intoxicating aromas of North Indian cooking. His hands work spices like an artist works a palette — instinctively, boldly, and always with a dash of something unexpected.

His Butter Chicken has been called "unfairly good" by the group, and his Palak Paneer convinced a few friends that spinach could actually be extraordinary. Dev cooks with heart and tradition, making every meal an event.

Signature Dishes: Butter Chicken, Palak Paneer, Chole Bhature

View Indian Recipes →
🇮🇳

Abu Shaik

The Flavor Architect — India

Abu is the Biryani king. Period. His Hyderabadi Biryani is a work of art — layers of fragrant rice, perfectly spiced meat, and saffron threads that tell a story centuries old.

Born into a family where food was the center of every celebration, Abu brings the rich Mughlai tradition to our table. His Tandoori Chicken could rival any restaurant, and his Samosas disappear before they even hit the plate.

Signature Dishes: Hyderabadi Biryani, Tandoori Chicken, Samosa

View Indian Recipes →
🇬🇷

Amalia Anna Vasilikou

The Mediterranean Muse — Greece

Amalia is the reason we all now know that olive oil isn't just an ingredient — it's a way of life. Raised in Greece, she learned to cook from her yiayia (grandmother), and every recipe carries that legacy.

Her Moussaka is a revelation, her Baklava makes grown adults weak in the knees, and her Greek Salad proves that perfection needs only the simplest ingredients. Amalia cooks with sunshine and sea salt.

Signature Dishes: Moussaka, Souvlaki, Spanakopita, Baklava, Greek Salad, Gyros

View Greek Recipes →
🇦🇱

Indrit Koka

The Balkan Treasure Keeper — Albania

Indrit is our gateway to one of Europe's most underrated cuisines. Albanian food is a beautiful crossroads of Mediterranean, Ottoman, and Balkan influences, and Indrit showcases this heritage with pride.

His Tavë Kosi — lamb baked in yogurt — is the dish that made everyone pause mid-bite and ask "what IS this?!" His Byrek is flaky perfection, and his Petulla with honey is the ultimate comfort food.

Signature Dishes: Tavë Kosi, Byrek, Fërgesë, Qofte, Flija, Petulla
